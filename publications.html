Research Themes

Phylogenetics

I am actively developing computational tools for phylogenetic analysis of single-cell data. My research in this area has involved both theoretical reconstruction guarantees, and the development of novel phylodynamic methods tailored to these systems.

Single-cell phylogenetics and phylodynamics.

N Mulberry*, J Pilarski*, J Dinger, T Stadler. Age-dependent phylodynamics with applciation to single-cell lineage trees. Molecular Biology and Evolution, 2026. (* denotes equal contribution)
N Mulberry and T Stadler. Strategies for resolving cellular phylogenies from sequential lineage tracing data. Theoretical Population Biology, 2026.
Software:
ADB: a BEAST2 package for phylodynamic inference under an age-dependent branching model.
scTreeSim (under development) : an R package for simulating single-cell data.

Epidemiology

I am interested in developing models to help explain patterns of disease spread, and to understand complex ecological dynamics of pathogens. A current area of research is to connect such models to genomic data. I have also been involved in more applied, public-health focused projects, particularly during the COVID-19 pandemic.
