About me

I am a mathematical modeller and computational biologist. My research focuses on developing mathematical models and statistical tools for understanding complex biological systems.

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at ETH in the Computational Evolution group, working with Prof. Tanja Stadler. Here, I have been developing phylogenetic and phylodynamic methods for analysing single-cell data.

I also have long-standing interest in infectious disease modelling and genomic epidemiology. I completed my PhD in Applied Mathematics with Prof. Caroline Colijn at Simon Fraser University.
