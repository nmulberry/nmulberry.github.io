About me

I am a mathematical modeller and computational biologist. My research focuses on developing mathematical models and statistical tools for understanding complex biological systems.

Currently, I am a postdoctoral researcher at ETH in the Computational Evolution group, working with Prof. Tanja Stadler. Here, I have been devloping phylogenetic and phylodynamic methods for analysing single-cell data.

Before my current role, I completed my PhD in Applied Mathematics at Simon Fraser University with Prof. Caroline Colijn. There, I developed mathematical models for understanding mechanisms of pathogen spread.
